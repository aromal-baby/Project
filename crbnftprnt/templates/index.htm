Carbon footprint regulations in Europe aim to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and combat climate change by promoting sustainable practices across industries. The European Union (EU) has implemented various legislative frameworks, such as the European Green Deal and the "Fit for 55" package, to achieve carbon neutrality by 2050. Key components include the EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS), which sets a cap on emissions and allows for trading carbon allowances, and the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM), which prevents carbon leakage by imposing tariffs on imported goods based on their carbon footprint. Additionally, directives like the Renewable Energy Directive and the Energy Efficiency Directive encourage the adoption of clean energy and energy-saving technologies. Companies are required to report their carbon emissions under the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD), while sectors like transportation and agriculture face specific emission reduction targets. Financial incentives, penalties, and subsidies for green initiatives further support compliance.
